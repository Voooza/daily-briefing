## 🤖 AI & Technology

### EU AI Act Omnibus — Final Stretch
The EU is finalizing the **AI Act Omnibus** package agreed on May 7. The Commission published the **Code of Practice on labeling AI-generated content** on June 10, and draft guidelines on classifying high-risk AI systems are open for public consultation until **June 23**. Formal adoption is expected by July 2026.

### NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Nano Omni
NVIDIA released **Nemotron 3 Nano Omni**, an open-source multimodal model unifying vision, audio, and language — claiming up to **9× higher throughput** than comparable open-weight models. The release signals NVIDIA's move to compete in the model-weights space alongside its chip dominance.

### Xiaomi MiMo-V2-Pro
Xiaomi's **MiMo-V2-Pro** is out, scoring **49 on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index**. The Chinese consumer-electronics giant continues investing heavily in frontier AI.

### Microsoft Scout
Microsoft unveiled **Scout**, an always-on AI personal agent woven directly into Microsoft 365, Teams, Outlook, OneDrive, and SharePoint — Microsoft's answer to the agentic AI wave reshaping the productivity software market.

### Pope's AI Ethics Encyclical
Pope Leo XIV published **"Magnifica Humanitas"**, his debut encyclical addressing AI ethics. He warns against technology concentrating power in a few hands and draws parallels to the labour disruptions of the Industrial Revolution.

### SpaceX IPO — Biggest in History
SpaceX listed on Nasdaq under ticker **SPCX** on June 12, raising **$75 billion** at $135/share — the largest IPO in stock market history, valuing the company at $1.75 trillion. Shares closed at ~**$161**, up 19% on the first day.

### Nvidia Eyes China Re-entry
Nvidia is quietly reopening commercial channels in China through its new **Vera CPU**, even as U.S. export regulations on AI chips tighten — a geopolitically sensitive move watched closely by Washington and Beijing alike.

## 🇨🇿 Czech News

### World Cup Opener: Czech Republic 1–2 South Korea
On June 12, Czechia fell to South Korea **2-1** in their opening **2026 FIFA World Cup** match. Ladislav Krejčí headed in an early lead from a long throw, but In-Beom Hwang equalized and Hyeon-Gyu Oh scored the winner. Prague pubs sold out viewing parties with CZK 300 entry fees.

**Next Czech match: June 18 vs. South Africa, kick-off 18:00.**

### Defense: Czech-Italy Ammunition Deal
Czechia and Italy finalized bilateral defense agreements this week covering **ammunition supply, joint production, and testing cooperation** — part of Czechia's broader NATO rearmament push.

### Budget Deficit Under Fire
Prime Minister Babiš's government is navigating a **310 billion korun budget deficit** — which the Czech Fiscal Council says exceeds legal limits. Despite this, macro fundamentals remain solid: May 2026 inflation at **2.1%**, unemployment low, and EU fund absorption accelerating.
