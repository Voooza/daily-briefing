## World Affairs

### Philippines: Deadly 7.8 Earthquake Strikes Mindanao
A powerful **magnitude 7.8 earthquake** struck offshore near Maasim, Sarangani, southern Philippines at 7:37 a.m. local time on Monday. At least **35 people are dead** and over 200 injured, with the toll still rising as rescue teams reach remote areas. A landslide triggered by the quake killed 13 villagers in Sarangani province. Small tsunamis hit nearby coasts, and waves were detected as far as Indonesia, Palau, and southern Japan. Phivolcs has recorded over **1,100 aftershocks**, including one measuring magnitude 6.7. Emergency response efforts are ongoing as power and communications remain disrupted across parts of Mindanao.

### Xi Jinping in Pyongyang — First Visit Since 2019
Chinese President **Xi Jinping** is on a two-day state visit to North Korea (June 8–9), his first trip to Pyongyang in seven years and his first overseas visit of 2026. He was received by **Kim Jong Un** at the airport with a 21-gun salute. Xi called for "powerful momentum" in bilateral ties and pledged expanded cooperation in economics, agriculture, science and technology. Analysts see the visit as China's attempt to reassert its central role over Pyongyang amid North Korea's deepening military ties with Russia and stalled denuclearization talks with the US.

### Iran–Israel War Reaches Day 100 — Ceasefire in Jeopardy
On the **100th day** of the Iran–Israel war (which began with US and Israeli strikes on Iran's leadership in February), Israel and Iran exchanged fresh strikes on Monday, putting the fragile April ceasefire in serious jeopardy. Iran launched missiles at Israel on Sunday; President Trump called Prime Minister Netanyahu urging restraint, but Israel responded regardless. Iran-backed **Houthi rebels** have also declared a ban on Israeli shipping in the Red Sea. Separately, the US refused to grant visas to **15 members of Iran's delegation** to the 2026 FIFA World Cup. Pakistan's interior minister is mediating, meeting Iran's foreign minister in Tehran over the weekend.

## Politics

### Peru: Sánchez Edges Ahead in Razor-Thin Presidential Runoff
With nearly **95% of votes counted** in Peru's presidential runoff, leftist **Roberto Sánchez** holds a paper-thin lead of **50.10% to 49.90%** over hard-right candidate **Keiko Fujimori**. Only a few thousand votes separate the two. International ballots — expected to favour Fujimori — have yet to be counted, keeping the race effectively undecided. Sánchez, a former trade minister and psychologist, ran on anti-poverty measures, police reform and a new constitution; Fujimori, a four-time candidate, pledged a 60-day state of emergency and tough-on-crime policies modelled on her father's presidency.

### Trump Eyes $250 Bill
President Trump could soon feature on a new **$250 denomination bill**, part of a broader Republican push to place the president's image on national institutions — the latest in a series of moves critics describe as blurring the line between the presidency and the state.

## Technology & AI

### Anthropic Releases Claude Opus 4.8
**Anthropic** launched **Claude Opus 4.8**, its new flagship model, delivering improved reasoning, enhanced agentic coding, effort control, dynamic workflows and a cheaper fast mode. Early benchmarks from Artificial Analysis rank it among the top models currently available. The release comes alongside OpenAI's **GPT-5.5 Instant** and Google's **Gemini 3.5 Flash**, signalling an accelerating race for frontier model performance.

### NVIDIA Cosmos 3: The First "Omnimodel" for Physical AI
**NVIDIA** unveiled **Cosmos 3**, described as the first fully open "omnimodel" for physical AI. It integrates vision reasoning, world simulation and action generation into a single mixture-of-transformers architecture — enabling AI to understand and interact with the physical world, with direct applications in robotics, autonomous vehicles and industrial automation.

### AI Moves from Demos to Business Infrastructure
Industry analysts note that AI is undergoing a decisive shift from headline-grabbing demos to **real business infrastructure**. Agentic systems are now automating research, coding, customer support, legal work and financial operations. Separately, **Orion-100B** demonstrated a 100-billion-parameter model can be trained for just **$1.25/hour**, and **MiniMax M3** cut per-token compute to 1/20th of prior models — pointing toward dramatically cheaper AI at scale.

## Climate & Science

### El Niño Warning: 18 Months of Compounding Risk Ahead
A **strong El Niño event** is forecast to develop in the tropical Pacific between June and September 2026, persisting into early 2027. The **Climate Vulnerable Forum** warns of compounding shocks to food systems, water security and public health, compounding an already record-warm global baseline.

### Great Nicobar: India's $9 Billion Megaproject
The Indian government is investing **$9 billion** to build a megaport, international airport and new city on **Great Nicobar Island** in the Bay of Bengal. Proponents say it could transform regional trade routes; critics warn of severe environmental risks to the remote island's pristine ecosystems and indigenous communities.
