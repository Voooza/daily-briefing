## 🤖 AI

**ChatGPT hits 1 billion users** — OpenAI's ChatGPT has crossed the symbolic milestone of one billion monthly active users. Its market share among major AI chatbots sits at 54.7% of worldwide web visits, though down from 76.5% in February 2025 as competition intensifies. Google Gemini is second at 27.4%, roughly doubling in six months.

**Goldman Sachs: $7.6 trillion in AI capex by 2031** — A new Goldman Sachs estimate puts cumulative AI capital expenditure from 2026–2031 at $7.6 trillion across compute, data centres, and power infrastructure — roughly 1.4× Germany's annual GDP. Physical AI and humanoid robotics are attracting billions from Germany to Barcelona.

**Jeff Bezos bets $12B on "artificial general engineer"** — Bezos is backing a company aiming to build AI that could redesign how jet engines, medicines, and other complex products are built from scratch.

**AI regulation wave incoming** — Colorado's Consumer Protection AI Act takes effect June 30 (risk management programs required for high-risk AI). The EU AI Act's full requirements for high-risk systems kick in August 2. Trump also signed a revised executive order directing federal agencies to develop classified benchmarks for assessing advanced AI cyber capabilities.

**Anthropic's Chris Olah on the Pope's AI encyclical** — Mechanistic interpretability researcher and Anthropic co-founder Chris Olah published his response to the Pope's encyclical on artificial intelligence.

## 🇨🇿 Czech News

**Ukrainian refugee protection extended** — Most EU countries backed a Czech-pushed proposal to extend temporary protection for Ukrainian refugees beyond 2027, but with restrictions for men of military age. Interior Minister Metnar confirmed the European Commission will bring a formal proposal by July.

**Czech National Bank tightening** — The CNB is requiring banks to set aside additional capital as a buffer against lending risk, a significant move as both business and household borrowing grows.

**Drone manufacturing in Kolín** — Ukrainian veterans are helping produce military drones at the U&C factory in Kolín, including reconnaissance, attack, and loitering variants with ranges exceeding 200 km.

**Havel Library leadership crisis** — Dagmar Havlová resigned from the Václav Havel Library board amid a donor exodus and leadership disputes.

## 🥊 MMA

**UFC Freedom 250 at the White House — Justin Gaethje is the new Lightweight Champion** — In a historic first, the UFC held an event on the South Lawn of the White House on June 14 to celebrate America's 250th anniversary. Justin Gaethje pulled off a stunning upset, dethroning the previously unbeaten Ilia Topuria when Topuria's corner threw in the towel before the fifth round. Gaethje controlled the fight after a tight opening round, battering Topuria to a bloody mess. All seven bouts on the card ended inside the distance.

**UFC's media rights revolution** — UFC announced a massive $7.7 billion exclusive deal with Paramount/CBS, abandoning the traditional pay-per-view model.

## 🚴 Cycling

**Tour de Suisse starts Wednesday** — The 89th Tour de Suisse (June 17–21) has been shortened from 8 days to 5 this year to better suit Tour de France contenders. **Tadej Pogačar** — fresh off dominating the Tour de Romandie — makes his TdS debut as the heavy favourite. He'll face Mathieu van der Poel, Tom Pidcock, and Primoz Roglic. The race opens in Sondrio, Italy and finishes in Villars-sur-Ollon, Switzerland.

**Copenhagen Sprint: Jasper Philipsen wins** — In chaotic wet conditions yesterday (June 14), Philipsen (Alpecin-Premier Tech) won the Copenhagen Sprint after a late crash split the peloton and Danny van Poppel (Red Bull-BORA) launched the sprint before Philipsen closed it out.
