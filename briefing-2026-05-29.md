# Daily Briefing — May 29, 2026

---

## World News

### Iran–US: Tentative Nuclear Deal Awaits Trump's Signature
US and Iranian negotiators have reached a 60-day memorandum of understanding (MOU) that would extend the ceasefire and launch formal nuclear talks — but President Trump has not yet signed off. Key terms include an Iranian commitment not to pursue nuclear weapons, full reopening of the Strait of Hormuz (all mines removed within 30 days, no tolls or harassment), and a proportional lifting of the US naval blockade. Vice President JD Vance said "a couple of language points" remain under discussion. Meanwhile, Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps launched an attack targeting a US air base, complicating the diplomatic picture. Markets were volatile on the conflicting signals, with oil prices swinging on each headline.

### Israel Intensifies Lebanon Offensive
Israel announced it will intensify strikes against Hezbollah in Lebanon. US forces also struck Iranian boats and missile launch sites as American envoys continued negotiations aimed at ending the three-month war. The dual-track of military escalation and diplomacy is generating significant uncertainty.

### Iran Restores Internet — Partially
Iranians began regaining internet access after authorities ended a months-long shutdown linked to wartime information controls. Service remains slow and patchy, and apps including YouTube and Instagram are heavily restricted. The partial restoration is being watched as a potential signal of regime confidence heading into a ceasefire.

### Ebola Outbreak: Congo
Health officials are tracking an Ebola outbreak centered in Mongbwalu, a gold-mining town of 130,000 in Ituri province, eastern Democratic Republic of Congo. The region's dense population, limited healthcare infrastructure, and ongoing regional conflict are complicating containment efforts.

### West Bank: Palestinian Humanitarian Conditions Worsen
Heightened Israeli movement restrictions continue to severely affect the 3.4 million Palestinians in the West Bank. Garbage has piled up in many areas following restrictions on municipal operations, adding a public health dimension to an already difficult humanitarian situation.

## Technology & AI

### Meta Launches Paid AI Subscription Tiers
Meta is rolling out Plus subscription plans for Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp globally and is piloting paid Meta AI tiers priced at **$7.99/month** and **$19.99/month**. The move marks a significant attempt to diversify revenue beyond advertising and signals Meta's intent to compete directly with ChatGPT Plus and Claude Pro in the consumer AI market.

### Chinese AI Labs Flood Coding Model Market
Four Chinese AI labs released competitive open-weights coding models within a **12-day window**, all reaching frontier-level performance on agentic engineering benchmarks at less than one-third the inference cost of Claude Opus 4.7. The coordinated release is increasing pressure on Western labs to reduce pricing and accelerate open-weights strategies.

### AI Is Now the Top Cyber Risk: WEF
A World Economic Forum report concludes that AI has overtaken every other force shaping the global cybersecurity landscape in 2026. **94% of organisations** now identify AI as the primary driver of cyber risk. Advanced LLMs can identify and exploit weaknesses in legacy infrastructure faster than most security teams can patch them. The European Central Bank separately warned that frontier AI is evolving faster than the financial sector can adapt.

### Snowflake's AI Bet Pays Off
Cloud data platform Snowflake surged **36.5%** — its best single day ever — after issuing strong fiscal Q2 guidance driven by AI workload demand. The result reinvigorated investor enthusiasm for the AI infrastructure trade after several days of mixed signals from the sector.

### Inflation Hotter Than Expected
The PCE price index — the Federal Reserve's preferred inflation measure — rose **3.8% year-over-year**, the highest reading in nearly three years and up sharply from 3.5% in March and 2.8% in February. The data raises questions about the Fed's path toward rate cuts and added mild pressure to bond markets.

### NIO Jumps 10% on ES9 Launch
Chinese EV maker NIO surged **10.45%** in Hong Kong trading (9.32% on US-listed shares) after officially launching its ES9 SUV. The jump reflects renewed investor appetite for Chinese EV plays following a period of sector consolidation.

### CEO Confidence Slides Back
The CEO Confidence Index fell from **59 to 47** in Q2 2026, reversing the sharp optimism surge seen in Q1. Executives cited geopolitical uncertainty, elevated inflation, and tightening credit conditions as the main headwinds.
