## Politics & Governance

### Senate Passes Immigration Enforcement Funding
After weeks of delays and fierce debate, the **U.S. Senate** passed legislation funding President Trump's immigration enforcement agencies, including key DHS operations. The bill cleared the chamber early Friday after a marathon vote-a-rama that saw a proposed DOJ funding restriction rejected.

### Trump Extends US–Iran Ceasefire
President **Donald Trump** announced an open-ended extension of the US–Iran ceasefire alongside a continuation of a U.S. naval blockade, pending the conclusion of ongoing negotiations. Despite repeated exchanges of fire, the White House maintains the ceasefire framework is holding.

### Israel–Lebanon: Ceasefire in Doubt
**Israel and Hezbollah** exchanged fire again overnight, casting fresh doubt on the fragile ceasefire brokered in Washington. Lebanese President **Joseph Aoun** called the agreement on implementing the truce "the last chance" for a comprehensive peace. The UN peacekeeping mission **UNIFIL** reported one peacekeeper killed by mortar fire in southeastern Lebanon.

## World Affairs

### Iran Fires Missiles Toward Kuwait and Bahrain
Iran launched **seven ballistic missiles** at Kuwait and Bahrain on June 5 — six were intercepted by U.S. and coalition forces, and the seventh failed to reach its target. Earlier in the week, Iranian drones damaged a terminal at Kuwait International Airport, killing one person. Iran's Revolutionary Guard disputed responsibility, but U.S. CENTCOM called those claims false. Kuwait suspended commercial flights following the attacks.

### Ukraine Strikes St. Petersburg; Russia Retaliates Massively
Ukrainian long-range drones flew more than **1,000 km** to strike an oil terminal in **St. Petersburg**, triggering a large blaze — the attack came days before Putin was due to host a major economic forum there. Russia retaliated with a massive overnight barrage: **656 drones and 73 missiles** hit Ukrainian cities, killing at least **22 people** and injuring over 130. Kyiv and Dnipro were primary targets. President Zelenskyy pledged further escalation of long-range strikes.

### North Korea Uranium Plant Spotted
State media photos from North Korea revealed what analysts assess is likely a facility for producing **weapons-grade uranium**, raising fresh alarm over Pyongyang's nuclear ambitions.

## Technology & AI

### Anthropic Confidentially Files for IPO at ~$965 Billion Valuation
**Anthropic** filed a confidential IPO prospectus with the SEC on June 1, following a **$65 billion Series H** funding round that lifted its valuation to approximately **$965 billion** — eclipsing OpenAI for the first time. Revenue hit a ~$47 billion annual run rate in May 2026, up from ~$10 billion a year prior. Bankers are targeting an **October 2026** listing, widely expected to debut above $1 trillion, opening what one analyst called "the floodgates for the IPO market."

### White House Executive Order Unleashes AI Innovation
The Trump administration issued an executive order on **June 2** designed to slash bureaucratic constraints on AI development and accelerate U.S. leadership. The order focuses on reducing regulatory friction for AI developers and researchers and signals a sharp pivot from the previous administration's cautious approach.

### New Frontier AI Models: Claude Opus 4.8, GPT-5.5 Instant, Gemini 3.5 Flash
Three major model releases landed this week:
- **Anthropic Claude Opus 4.8** — new flagship with improved reasoning, agentic coding, effort control, and top benchmark results
- **OpenAI GPT-5.5 Instant** — faster inference at lower cost
- **Google Gemini 3.5 Flash** — frontier-level intelligence at 4× the speed of comparable models

### SK Hynix Crosses $1 Trillion Market Cap
South Korean chipmaker **SK Hynix** reached a **$1 trillion market capitalization** for the first time, joining Samsung and Micron as surging AI memory demand reshapes the global semiconductor landscape.

### AI Agents Take Center Stage
Enterprise adoption of **agentic AI** — systems that can plan, use tools, and complete multi-step tasks autonomously — is accelerating. Analysts note faster lead research, support triage, and product operations as primary use cases, though consumer uptake remains slow: only **3% of U.S. households** currently pay for personal AI subscriptions.
