## 🤖 AI & Technology

### June 2026 — Busiest Model Release Month Yet
June 2026 is shaping up as the most active AI model release month of the year. Recent major releases include:
- **Gemini 3.5 Flash** from Google
- **Claude Opus 4.8** with 1 million token default context and dynamic workflows
- **GPT-5.5** with instant/thinking/pro modes

### ChatGPT Market Share Slips Below 50% (For First Time)
ChatGPT's global share of AI chatbot web visits fell below 50% for the first time ever. Google Gemini now sits at **27.4%** — up ~104% in six months. The competitive landscape is reshaping fast.

### Alphabet Loses $269B in Market Cap
Alphabet shed approximately **$269 billion in market cap** between June 18–24 following a wave of senior Google DeepMind departures. Notable names — Noam Shazeer, John Jumper, Jonas Adler, and Alexander Pritzel — all headed to OpenAI or Anthropic.

### GPT-5.6 Launch Delayed
Prediction market odds for a GPT-5.6 release in the June 22–28 window collapsed from ~83% to ~18%. The Trump administration also requested OpenAI limit the rollout to a select group of trusted partners, with government agencies approving access customer-by-customer.

### Meta AI Restructuring — 8,000 Layoffs
Meta began implementing layoffs of approximately **8,000 employees** as part of an AI-focused restructuring, while simultaneously reassigning **7,000 more employees** to AI-focused teams.

### Samsung's $648B Decade-Long Bet
Samsung Group plans to announce a decade-long investment of **1,000 trillion won (~$648 billion)** in South Korea covering chip factories, AI data centers, batteries, and displays.

### Colorado AI Consumer Protection Act — June 30
The **Colorado Consumer Protections for Artificial Intelligence Act** takes effect Monday June 30. It covers employment, healthcare, housing, finance, and legal services — requiring risk management programs, annual impact assessments, disclosure when AI drives consequential decisions, and a right to appeal for affected Colorado residents.

### Cybersecurity
Russian authorities reportedly used **Cellebrite** phone extraction technology to access a dissident's device — months after the Israeli company said it stopped doing business in Russia over human rights concerns.

## 🇨🇿 Czech News

### Record-Breaking Heatwave This Weekend
Czechia is in the grip of a severe heatwave. Tropical nights are recorded across **a fifth of all weather stations**, with central Prague staying above 23°C overnight. The Czech Hydrometeorological Institute warns that the all-time Czech temperature record of **40.4°C (set in 2012)** could be broken both Saturday and Sunday.

### Wildfire Alert
Czech meteorologists have issued wildfire warnings across **Bohemia, Olomouc, and southern Moravia** through the weekend, citing warm and dry conditions.

### Prague Hotel Fire
A fire broke out on a rooftop terrace at a hotel on **Hybernská Street** in central Prague on Thursday evening. More than **200 people were evacuated** and surrounding streets were closed.

### Constitutional Crisis: President vs. PM Over NATO Summit
The standoff between **President Petr Pavel** and **PM Andrej Babiš** over who attends the NATO summit in Ankara (July 7–8) has reached — and been resolved by — the **Constitutional Court**. After Babiš excluded Pavel from the Czech delegation, the Court issued an interim measure ordering the government to facilitate Pavel's participation. The deeper clash is over Czech foreign policy direction, NATO spending (CZ will again miss the 2% GDP target), and alignment on the Ukraine war.

### UNICEF Venezuela Appeal
The Czech Committee for UNICEF launched a fundraising campaign following this week's earthquake in Venezuela, supporting affected children and families.
