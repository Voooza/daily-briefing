## 🤖 AI & Technology

### Apple Sues OpenAI Over Trade Secrets — Biggest Story
Apple filed suit against OpenAI in the Northern District of California (~July 10), alleging systematic trade-secret theft "at every level" tied to Apple's hardware plans. The complaint names OpenAI hardware chief **Tang Tan** (a former Apple VP) and a former Apple employee accused of stealing a laptop. OpenAI denies wrongdoing. Elon Musk publicly piled on against Sam Altman over the weekend of July 11–12.

### Model Wars Heat Up
- **Anthropic** is re-extending access to **Claude Fable 5** in what's being read as a direct response to OpenAI's new Sol model. Separately, **Claude Sonnet 5** (launched June 30) became the default model for all Free/Pro users on July 1, offering near-Opus-4.8 performance; introductory API pricing is $2/$10 per M tokens (in/out) through August 31, rising to $3/$15 after.
- Anthropic researchers described **"J-Space"** — an internal workspace Claude appears to use to manipulate ideas non-verbally, detected via Jacobian-based analysis — drawing comparisons to human conscious thought.
- **OpenAI** launched **GPT-5.6** on July 9, plus a "ChatGPT Work" tier and a preview of flagship **GPT-5.6 Sol** (with Terra/Luna variants) for developers and enterprise. It also shipped **GPT-Live**, a full-duplex voice AI that listens, speaks, and reasons simultaneously with real-time translation and live search.
- **Google's Gemini 3.5 Pro** has been delayed to **July 17** for a full architectural rebuild scrapping the old 2.5 Pro base; Gemini 3.5 Flash remains the current broadly-available release.
- **xAI** shipped **Grok 4.5** on July 8 — its first release since acquiring Cursor — priced at $2/$6 per M tokens, alongside a completed "Grok Imagine" image/video generator.

### Big Tech Moves
- Google, Microsoft, Salesforce, Snowflake, and ServiceNow agreed on a shared **AI agent-to-enterprise-data protocol**, positioned as a rival standard to Anthropic/OpenAI approaches.
- **Meta**: Zuckerberg told staff (July 2) AI agent progress "hasn't really accelerated" as hoped, even as Meta shipped Muse Image and previewed Muse Video. Its custom **"Iris" AI chip** (with Broadcom/TSMC) begins production in September, targeting 14GW capacity by 2027; the stock had its best week since 2024 (+15%).
- **Nvidia** released Nemotron 3 Ultra (July 8) and denied reports its Kyber NVL144 platform is delayed to 2028 — the stock is still ~28% below its 52-week high.

### Policy & Security
- Illinois Gov. Pritzker signed the **AI Safety Measures Act** (July 6), requiring safety disclosures, incident reporting, and independent audits. As of July 1, US states have 109 AI laws and 28 data-center laws on the books.
- The FTC issued a policy statement targeting state laws that force AI "output alteration"; comment period runs through July 31.
- **Accenture confirmed a breach** (~35GB of source code, keys and tokens stolen); **Progress Software** urged ShareFile customers to shut down Storage Zone Controller servers over a "credible external security threat."
- US VC funding hit **$412.7B in H1 2026** — up 30% versus all of 2025 — with AI startups accounting for 86% of that total.

## 🇨🇿 Czech News

### Babiš–Pavel Rift Deepens
PM **Andrej Babiš** rejected President **Petr Pavel's** proposal to resume regular top-official foreign-policy consultations, saying he sees no reason to coordinate with a president "running a campaign against him" — after previously calling Pavel's NATO summit attendance in Ankara "a disgrace." The two traveled to the Ankara summit separately. Babiš also reversed his earlier "not a single crown" stance and agreed to help fund a US-backed program purchasing weapons for Ukraine.

### Defense Budget Jumps
The government will add **CZK 36 billion** to the 2027 defense budget, pushing spending to **2% of GDP** — the NATO target — for the first time.

### Severe Weather Warning
CHMI issued a **level-2 warning for severe thunderstorms** covering the entire country for Tuesday, July 15 (11:00–22:00), with up to ~50mm of rain, hail to 2cm, and wind gusts up to 90 km/h.

### Economy
June inflation slowed to **1.5% y/y** (below the 1.8% forecast), though services inflation stays hot at 4.5%. The CNB's key rate sits at 3.75% after a June hike driven by strong wage growth (8% in Q1) and fiscal/geopolitical risk. Separately, **Zetor Tractors** is ending 80 years of tractor production in Brno, shifting manufacturing to India while keeping HQ and R&D in Brno.

### Sports/Culture
**Linda Nosková** beat compatriot **Karolína Muchová** in an all-Czech Wimbledon final, becoming the youngest women's champion in 15 years.

## 🥊 MMA / UFC

### McGregor's Comeback Ends in 69 Seconds
At **UFC 329** (July 11, T-Mobile Arena, Las Vegas), **Max Holloway** TKO'd **Conor McGregor** just 69 seconds into round 1 of their rematch — McGregor blew out his knee landing awkwardly from a jumping roundhouse kick and couldn't bear weight. Dana White suspects a torn ACL, though a reviewing doctor thinks a meniscus tear is more likely. McGregor has confirmed he'll have surgery and intends to fight out the final bout on his UFC contract, rejecting retirement calls.

### Other UFC 329 Results
Ryan Gandra def. Zach Reese (TKO, Rd 1); Alessandro Costa def. Cody Durden (submission, Rd 2). Cory Sandhagen is awaiting MRI results after a submission attempt hobbled him mid-fight.

### Key Storylines
- **Stage 6 (Col du Tourmalet):** Teammate Isaac del Toro launched Pogačar 5km from the summit; Pogačar soloed 43km to the finish, dropping Vingegaard by 2'42" — a gap that has held ever since — in what's being called a potentially race-defining move.
- **Stage 9** was shortened from 185.5km to 155.5km due to a red heatwave alert in Corrèze; Mathieu van der Poel won a 4-up sprint from the breakaway ahead of Tobias Halland Johannessen and Tom Pidcock, with GC unchanged.
- **Crashes:** Torstein Træen (Uno-X), an early yellow-jersey holder, abandoned after a Tourmalet descent crash with concussion and fractured ribs. Alex Molenaar (Caja Rural) also abandoned after a Stage 5 crash.
- A serious incident near the Stage 9 finish (~July 12): a race-convoy car driver suffered a medical episode and crashed into spectators, injuring eight, one seriously.
- **Paul Seixas**, 19, is the breakout story of the race — 6th overall in his Grand Tour debut, having already won Flèche Wallonne and Itzulia Basque Country this season.
