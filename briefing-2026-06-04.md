## World News

### Ukraine: Drones Strike St. Petersburg Oil Terminal, Russia Pounds Kyiv
Overnight, Ukrainian drones struck and set ablaze **Russia's largest oil terminal on the Baltic Sea in St. Petersburg** — the attack was carried out as the city hosted Putin's annual St. Petersburg International Economic Forum. Separately, Russian forces launched a **massive aerial assault on Kyiv and other Ukrainian cities**, killing at least 22 civilians and wounding 138 more. Ukrainian air defences intercepted 189 of 198 drones deployed during the overnight wave. On the ground, ISW data for the past four weeks shows Russian forces recorded a **net loss of 93 square miles** of Ukrainian territory, as the Russian spring-summer offensive has largely been halted. A military plant in the Tambov region was also struck by Ukrainian drones.

### Iran Escalation: Kuwait Airport Hit, US Retaliates on Qeshm Island
Building on Tuesday's exchange, **Iranian drones heavily damaged the passenger terminal at Kuwait International Airport**, killing one person, wounding dozens, and temporarily closing the facility. Iran also fired missiles at Bahrain — all intercepted by US and Bahraini air-defence systems. The US military responded with strikes on an Iranian military facility on **Qeshm Island** in the Strait of Hormuz. Diplomatic back-channels remain open but fragile; Iran's foreign ministry separately announced cryptocurrency-exchange sanctions targeting the US. The Israel-Lebanon 45-day ceasefire extension agreed on June 3 remains in effect.

### Albania: Protests Against Kushner-Linked Coastal Development
Police blocked streets in Tirana on Wednesday as demonstrators opposed a **luxury coastal development project linked to Jared Kushner and Ivanka Trump**. Critics cite environmental damage and a lack of transparency in how the Albanian government approved the project; the government maintains the development will transform the nation's tourism economy.

## US Domestic

### US Military Drug-Boat Strikes Top 200 Dead Since September
The Trump administration's campaign of **military strikes on alleged drug-smuggling vessels** in the eastern Pacific has now killed more than 200 people since operations began in September 2025. The most recent strike, on June 3, killed two men. Critics — including legal scholars and Latin American governments — question the strikes' legality and the administration's evidence for classifying targets as "narco-terrorists." The White House continues to frame the operation as essential to stemming fentanyl flow into the US.

### Florida Sues OpenAI Over Child Safety
**Florida became the first US state to file suit against OpenAI and CEO Sam Altman**, alleging the company knowingly allows ChatGPT to expose minors to harmful content despite internal awareness of the risks. The suit adds to a mounting legal pile for OpenAI, which is also navigating antitrust scrutiny and a pending IPO.

### Trump Signs AI Early-Access Executive Order
President Trump signed an executive order directing AI companies to **voluntarily submit their most powerful models to the US government for testing up to 30 days before public release**. Federal agencies are instructed to develop benchmarks for assessing AI models' cybersecurity capabilities and to stand up an "AI Cybersecurity Clearinghouse" for sharing vulnerability information. The order broadly favours industry-led standards over mandatory regulation.

## Technology & AI

### Sanders Introduces AI Sovereign Wealth Fund Bill
Senator **Bernie Sanders** formally introduced the American AI Sovereign Wealth Fund Act, which would impose a **one-time 50% tax — paid in stock — on OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI, and other large AI companies** to seed a publicly owned fund. The fund would hold voting shares, have equal board representation, and distribute proceeds directly to Americans. The bill faces long odds in the current Senate but has sparked significant debate ahead of Anthropic's anticipated IPO.

### Broadcom Miss Rattles AI Hardware Narrative
**Broadcom reported fiscal Q2 revenue below analyst expectations**, sending shares down nearly 14% in after-hours trading — a notable reversal for one of the key beneficiaries of the AI infrastructure wave. CrowdStrike fell more than 11% after issuing soft Q2 sales guidance. The pair weighed on futures and set a cautious tone for Thursday's open.
