## World Affairs

### U.S. Strikes Iran in Strait of Hormuz
The U.S. military launched what it called "defensive" strikes on missile launch sites in southern Iran after Iranian boats were observed laying mines in the Strait of Hormuz. U.S. Central Command stated the strikes were aimed at protecting American troops from Iranian forces. Iran's FIFA World Cup training camp has since relocated from the U.S. to Mexico, citing the ongoing conflict and security concerns.

### Iran Lifts Internet Restrictions
Separately, Iranian President Masoud Peshekian ordered the rollback of nearly three months of restrictions on public internet access — a notable civil concession in the midst of the military tensions.

### Ebola Outbreak Worsening in Central Africa
The WHO warned that the Ebola outbreak spanning the Democratic Republic of the Congo and Uganda is spreading faster than containment efforts can keep up. WHO Director-General Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus said the situation will likely worsen before it improves. At least 220 suspected deaths have been linked to the outbreak so far.

### Baku–Tbilisi Rail Revived
Passenger rail service resumed between Azerbaijan's capital Baku and Georgia's capital Tbilisi after a six-year suspension, with the first train completing the route on Monday.

### Hajj 2026 Underway
Muslim pilgrims gathered at Mount of Mercy on the plain of Arafat near Mecca for the annual Hajj pilgrimage.

## Technology & AI

### Google Launches Gemini 3.5 Flash
Google unveiled Gemini 3.5 Flash at Google I/O 2026, describing it as the first in a new series combining frontier-level intelligence with fast, action-oriented responses — rival to large flagship models in quality but at Flash speeds.

### $200B Cloud Infrastructure Push
A major infrastructure commitment of over $200 billion was announced for cloud and chip development, largely in collaboration with Google Cloud — signalling continued massive investment in AI compute.

### AI Regulation Advancing
The U.S. government is aggressively pushing for pre-release access to AI models. Microsoft and xAI are among the companies reportedly agreeing to give regulators early model access. The rise of the Chief AI Officer (CAIO) role has accelerated sharply: 76% of surveyed organizations have now established the position, up from just 26% in 2025.

### AI Dominates Cybersecurity Findings
For the first time, the majority of security vulnerability findings in May came from frontier AI models scanning code — 26 CVEs covering 75 issues, compared to a typical monthly volume of fewer than 5 CVEs.
