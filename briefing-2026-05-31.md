# Daily Briefing — May 31, 2026

---

## World News

### Iran–US: Ceasefire Holds, Deal Still Awaited
President Trump is weighing a tentative agreement that would open the Strait of Hormuz and launch formal nuclear talks with Iran, but has not yet given a public indication of a decision. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth, speaking at the Shangri-La Dialogue in Singapore, stated that US forces are "more strongly placed" to resume combat in the Persian Gulf than on day one of the conflict — a signal the military option remains firmly on the table. Hegseth also addressed China, NATO and Taiwan, underlining the summit's broad significance for US foreign policy.

### Israel Advances into Southern Lebanon
Israeli forces crossed the Litani River and reached Nabatieh, one of southern Lebanon's largest cities — the deepest Israeli ground push into Lebanon since 2006. The advance follows sustained strikes on Hezbollah positions and raises questions about the scope of Israel's campaign and international calls for restraint.

### Drone Strikes Zaporizhzhia Nuclear Plant
A drone strike hit a turbine building at Ukraine's Zaporizhzhia nuclear power plant on Saturday, punching a hole in the wall according to the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA). No radiation release was reported, but the incident heightens long-standing international concern over the safety of Europe's largest nuclear facility amid the ongoing conflict.

### Ebola Outbreak: WHO Chief Visits Congo
WHO Director-General Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus travelled to the Ebola-hit Ituri province in the Democratic Republic of Congo, urging residents to seek treatment early and observe safe burial practices. More than 1,000 suspected cases have now been reported; aid groups warn the response is falling behind the pace of spread and are calling for greater international funding.

### US to Speed Up European Troop Withdrawal
The United States is planning to accelerate the withdrawal of troops from its European bases and will present formal proposals to NATO allies next month, according to Germany's *Welt am Sonntag*. The move is expected to trigger significant debate within the alliance about burden-sharing and European strategic autonomy.

### PSG Retains Champions League Title
Paris Saint-Germain defeated Arsenal on penalties after a 1–1 draw to retain the UEFA Champions League. Celebrations in Paris were marred by clashes with police, resulting in more than 400 arrests across France — 283 in the capital alone.

### Virginia Highway Bus Crash
At least 5 people were killed and nearly 50 injured when a bus plowed into multiple vehicles near a work zone on Interstate 95 in Virginia, one of the deadliest highway incidents in the US this year.

## US Politics

### Federal Judge Upholds Trump Mail-In Voting Order
A federal judge in Washington, D.C. declined to temporarily block President Trump's executive order calling for restrictions on mail-in voting, allowing it to remain in effect pending further litigation. Critics argue the order will suppress turnout ahead of upcoming elections.

### Minnesota GOP Convention Backs Qualls for Governor
Minnesota Republicans endorsed Kendall Qualls as their gubernatorial candidate at the state party convention, though competitor Lisa Demuth raised concerns about vote validity, signalling a potentially contested primary season.

## Technology & AI

### Google Launches Gemini 3.5 Flash and Gemini Spark
Google unveiled Gemini 3.5 Flash — the first model in its new series combining "frontier intelligence with action" — now generally available via the Gemini API, Android Studio, and the new **Google Antigravity** agent development platform. Alongside it, Google introduced **Gemini Spark**, a 24/7 personal AI agent designed to operate autonomously in the background on phones and laptops.

### Governments Push for Pre-Release AI Access
The US government is pushing major AI companies — including Microsoft and Elon Musk's xAI — to provide regulators with early access to models before public release. The move signals a shift toward proactive AI oversight and could reshape how frontier models are deployed.

### Anthropic Opens Milan Office
Anthropic expanded its European footprint with a new office in Milan, its second European hub after London, serving Italy's enterprise, research, and developer communities. The opening comes within 48 hours of another international office launch, reflecting the company's accelerating global expansion.

### Dell's AI Server Revenue Surges 757%
Dell Technologies reported AI server revenue of **$16.1 billion** — a **757% year-over-year** increase — driving its fastest revenue growth since its 2018 return to public markets. Dell stock surged **32%**, its best single day ever, energising the broader AI infrastructure trade.

### AMD Ships 6th-Gen EPYC on TSMC 2nm
AMD has begun production of its 6th Generation EPYC "Venice" processors, manufactured on TSMC's cutting-edge **2nm process** — a significant leap for AI data-centre workloads that will intensify competition with Intel and NVIDIA in the infrastructure market.

### Light-Matter Particle Could Slash AI Energy Use
Researchers at the University of Pennsylvania have created a hybrid light-matter particle — a polariton — that could dramatically accelerate AI computing while using a fraction of the energy of current silicon-based approaches. The research is at an early stage but has attracted interest from semiconductor labs.

### Inflation Remains Sticky
CPI rose to **3.3% year-over-year** in the latest reading (up from 2.4% in February), with headline CPI up **0.9% month-over-month** driven by a **21.2% surge in gasoline prices** — largely attributable to Middle East supply disruption. The Federal Reserve held the federal funds rate steady at **3.50–3.75%** for the third consecutive meeting.

### Q1 GDP at 2.0%
First-quarter GDP growth came in at approximately **2.0% annualised**, a modest improvement on the prior quarter but slightly below expectations — reflecting an economy that continues to expand at a measured pace amid geopolitical headwinds.

### Kevin Warsh Advances for Fed Chair
The Senate Banking Committee advanced Kevin Warsh's nomination as the next Federal Reserve Chair, setting up a full Senate confirmation vote ahead of Jerome Powell's term ending May 15.

### IEA Cuts Oil Demand Outlook
The International Energy Agency revised its 2026 global oil demand forecast, projecting a Q2 contraction of roughly **1.5 million barrels per day** — which would be the sharpest demand decline since the COVID-19 pandemic — driven by the Middle East conflict's impact on supply chains and consumer sentiment.
