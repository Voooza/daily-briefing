# Daily Briefing — June 1, 2026

## World News

### Middle East: US Bombs Qeshm & Goruk; Kuwait Hit by Missiles
The conflict in the region continued to escalate overnight. The US military struck Iranian radar and drone infrastructure on the island of Qeshm and in the city of Goruk. Kuwait separately reported "hostile" missile and drone attacks, with Iran's IRGC claiming a retaliatory strike on a base used for attacks on its Sirik Island. France is pushing for an emergency UN Security Council meeting over Israel's expanding invasion of Lebanon, which has now displaced over **1 million people** (more than 20% of the population). Lebanon's PM has described the Israeli campaign as collective punishment. Iran says diplomatic talks with the US are still ongoing, but a deal remains in limbo — Trump has insisted Iran must agree never to acquire a nuclear weapon.

### US-Iran Nuclear Deal: Still No Signature
Despite a tentative memorandum of understanding reportedly agreed last week, President Trump has not signed off. The White House says "a couple of language points" remain unresolved. Bond yields fell and oil prices retreated on hopes of a resolution, but markets remain volatile on every headline out of the region.

### Clashes Outside New Jersey ICE Facility
Supporters of immigrant detainees and pro-ICE demonstrators faced off outside Delaney Hall in Newark, New Jersey. Local and state authorities imposed a curfew to prevent further escalation. The standoff reflects ongoing national tensions over immigration enforcement policy.

### Colombia Heads to Election Runoff
Colombia's presidential election was held Sunday; no candidate secured an outright majority, sending the race to a June runoff. The outcome will have significant implications for regional trade, drug policy, and relations with Venezuela.

### California Governor's Primary — Democrats at Risk
With 61 candidates on the June 2 primary ballot, analysis suggests Democrats could be shut out of the general election in California under the state's top-two primary system — a remarkable development in the country's most populous state heading into midterms.

## Technology & AI

### Google Launches Gemini 3.5 Flash — Now Powering AI Search for 1 Billion Users
Gemini 3.5 Flash is now generally available, delivering frontier-level intelligence at **4× the speed** of comparable models at $1.50 / $9 per 1M tokens with a 1M token context window. Google simultaneously announced it has surpassed **1 billion monthly users** on AI Mode in Search, upgrading the default model to Gemini 3.5 Flash globally. AI Mode query volume has been **doubling every quarter** since launch.

### Claude Opus 4.8 Benchmarks Released
Anthropic published performance figures for Claude Opus 4.8: **88.6%** on SWE-bench Verified, **74.6%** on Terminal-Bench 2.1, and **1890 Elo** on GDPval-AA. The model supports parallel subagent workflows and a 2.5× fast mode, pushing the frontier on complex agentic engineering tasks.

### Google I/O 2026: 100 Announcements
Google's annual developer conference wrapped with over 100 product announcements centered on AI integration across Search, Android, Chrome, and Workspace. SynthID — Google's content authentication tool for AI-generated images, video, and audio — has been used **50 million times** globally and is expanding to Search and Chrome.

### AI 3D Asset Startup Vast Hits $1B Valuation
Beijing-based Vast, which uses AI to generate 3D assets from text and image prompts, raised approximately **$200M** at a valuation exceeding **$1 billion**. The company reports 20 million global users, highlighting growing demand for AI-native creative tooling.

### Pancreatic Cancer: Novel Pill Shows Promise
Researchers reported Sunday that a new oral drug significantly extended survival for patients with advanced pancreatic cancer — one of the deadliest and hardest-to-treat cancer types. The trial results are being called a meaningful step forward in a disease that has seen very little progress in decades.
