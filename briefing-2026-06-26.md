## 🤖 AI & Technology

### Fable 5 Still Dark — Day 14

Anthropic's **Fable 5 and Mythos 5 remain suspended** as of June 25. The US government's June 12 export-control directive ordered a full suspension of both models for any foreign national — inside or outside the US, including Anthropic's own foreign-national employees. Because Anthropic cannot filter real-time API traffic by nationality across all its enterprise contracts and delivery paths, it chose a **global shutdown** rather than risk non-compliance. No official restoration date has been given. All other Claude models remain available.

Canadian PM **Mark Carney** cited the outage at G7 discussions, warning countries against over-dependence on a handful of AI providers and calling for greater supplier diversification.

### OpenAI Partner Network Launches

OpenAI launched the **OpenAI Partner Network** — a formal, tiered (Select / Advanced / Elite) ecosystem for consultants and integrators backed by **$150 million**, with an ambition to certify up to **300,000 consultants** by year-end. The move signals OpenAI's push to deepen enterprise penetration beyond direct API access.

### ChatGPT Hits 1 Billion Monthly Active Users

ChatGPT passed the **1 billion MAU** milestone, cementing its position as the world's most-used AI application. Its share of worldwide AI chatbot visits sits at ~54.7% (down from 76.5% in Feb 2025), with **Google Gemini surging to 27.4%** — up ~104% over six months.

### Google Held Liable for False AI Overviews

A Munich court ruled that **Google can be held legally liable** for factually incorrect statements made in its AI Overviews, treating AI-generated answers as Google's own speech rather than neutral search results. The decision could have wide-ranging consequences for AI search products across Europe.

### AI PCs Gain Momentum

Hardware vendors are aggressively pushing **AI PCs** with on-device neural processing units (NPUs) — including Nvidia's RTX Spark — capable of running AI agents and assistants locally without cloud dependency. The category is widely expected to dominate the second half of 2026 PC refresh cycles.

### Meta's Wearable AI Ambitions

Meta is testing an **AI-powered pendant** and a **"Wearables for Work"** business line alongside its expanding Ray-Ban AI glasses portfolio. The company plans to significantly scale wearable device sales before year-end.

### Trump Signs AI & Cybersecurity Executive Order

President Trump signed a revised **Executive Order on AI Innovation and Security**, directing federal agencies to strengthen cybersecurity capabilities, establish a classified AI model benchmarking process, and create a national cybersecurity clearinghouse.

## 🇨🇿 Czech News

### Public Media Strike

Staff at **Czech Television (ČT) and Czech Radio (ČRo)** staged a one-day warning strike, centered on ČT headquarters in Prague. Employees are protesting a government plan to place public media funding under direct government control, which would cut around **CZK 1.4 billion** and eliminate 450–700 of the institutions' 4,250 jobs. A major public solidarity rally took place the day before.

### Babiš Pushes Skyfox Funding onto Europe

PM Andrej Babiš is lobbying European allies that donated F-16s to Ukraine to co-finance the purchase of **10 Czech L-39NG Skyfox training aircraft** for Ukraine. Estimated cost: **CZK 5.3–6.3 billion**; earliest delivery 2028–2029.

### NATO Summit Dispute

The Czech government held an emergency cabinet session to resolve a months-long standoff between PM Babiš and President **Petr Pavel** over who will formally represent the Czech Republic at the **July NATO summit in Ankara**.

### Dancing House Turns 30

Prague's iconic **Dancing House** (Tančící dům) celebrated its **30th anniversary**. Designed by Frank Gehry and Vlado Milunić and completed in 1996, it remains one of the most recognizable pieces of contemporary architecture in Central Europe.

### Heat Wave & Storms

A high-pressure system over central Europe is driving temperatures back up in Czechia after a brief cool spell. Below-average rainfall is accelerating **drought conditions**, while the Czech Hydrometeorological Institute has issued storm warnings for parts of Bohemia and Moravia.

### Missing Hiker Found in Cave

A **74-year-old German tourist** missing for two days in Bohemian Switzerland was found alive — exhausted but uninjured — inside a cave near **Dravčí skály** after a major multi-agency search operation.

## 🏀 NBA — 2026 Draft Night

### AJ Dybantsa Goes #1 Overall to the Wizards

The **Washington Wizards** opened the 2026 NBA Draft (June 25) by selecting **AJ Dybantsa** from BYU with the first overall pick — the franchise's first #1 pick since **John Wall in 2010**. Dybantsa, 19, led the nation with **25.5 PPG** at BYU, won the Julius Erving Award, and shattered Danny Ainge's 48-year-old BYU freshman scoring record with a 43-point game.

### Draft Night Trade: Knicks, Lakers, Mavs, Suns

The Knicks, Lakers, Mavericks, and Suns completed a multi-team pick swap on draft night. New York used the deal to add another first-round selection as the defending champions continue to build around their core.

### College Freshmen Dominate Top 10

The first round was notable for the depth of college freshmen talent, with players from the Big 12 and Michigan leading the way. The Draft marks its **80th year**.

## 🥊 MMA / UFC

### UFC Fight Night 280 Baku — Tomorrow (June 27)

**UFC Fight Night: Fiziev vs. Torres** takes place Saturday at the **National Gymnastics Arena in Baku, Azerbaijan** — the first UFC event held in the country. The card streams on Paramount+.

**Main Event — Lightweight:** Rafael Fiziev (AZ/KZ) vs. Manuel Torres (MX, #15 ranked)
- Fiziev is the more diverse and technical striker, bringing a Muay Thai-based arsenal; he's won just one of his last five but is fighting on home soil.
- Torres is a pure finisher — all five UFC wins have come by stoppage, every one earning a Performance of the Night bonus.
- Expect chaotic, high-volume striking exchanges.

**Prelims:** 9:00 AM ET | **Main Card:** 12:00 PM ET

### Meta UFC Rankings Replace Media Voting

On June 20, the UFC rolled out the **Meta UFC Rankings** — an Elo-based mathematical model that replaces the previous media-voting system. The new rankings prioritize objective win/loss data and opponent quality over journalist opinion.

### Paramount+ Deal Kicks In

Starting in 2026, all **UFC events stream exclusively on Paramount+** at no additional pay-per-view charge — 13 flagship events and 30 Fight Nights per year, as part of a seven-year deal.
