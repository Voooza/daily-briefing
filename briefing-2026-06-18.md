## 🤖 AI & Technology

### White House AI Executive Order
President Trump signed an Executive Order on June 2 directing federal agencies to strengthen cybersecurity capabilities using AI, establish a cybersecurity clearinghouse, and create a classified benchmarking process for advanced AI models. The goal: keep the US as the global leader in AI.

### Colorado AI Consumer Protection Law Takes Effect June 30
Colorado's Consumer Protections for Artificial Intelligence Act kicks in at end of June. It covers deployers and developers of high-risk AI in employment, healthcare, finance, education, housing, and legal services — requiring risk management programs, annual impact assessments, and disclosure obligations.

### ChatGPT vs Gemini: Market Share Shifts
ChatGPT holds 54.7% of web visits across the 7 largest AI chatbots (down from 76.5% in Feb 2025). Google Gemini is second at 27.4%, up ~104% in six months.

### Meta's AI Restructuring
Meta is laying off ~8,000 employees as part of an AI-focused restructuring, while reassigning 7,000 more to AI-focused teams. They're also testing an AI pendant wearable and launching "Wearables for Work" glasses.

### SpaceX IPO — Largest in History
SpaceX priced its IPO at $135/share, raising ~$75 billion — the largest IPO ever, valuing the company at around $1.77 trillion.

### Alphabet's $80B Stock Offering
Alphabet is raising $80 billion to fund massive investments in AI infrastructure, data centers, and compute capacity.

### Florida Sues OpenAI
Florida became the first US state to sue OpenAI and CEO Sam Altman, alleging ChatGPT contributed to harmful incidents involving users.

### AI PCs on the Rise
Hardware vendors are pushing AI PCs with neural processing units (NPUs) and new chips like NVIDIA's RTX Spark, enabling local AI processing without the cloud.

## 🇨🇿 Czech Republic

### PM Babiš on US-Iran Agreement
Czech PM Andrej Babiš (ANO) cautiously welcomed a US-Iran agreement aimed at ending their conflict. President Pavel called for more informal NATO talks to avoid provoking the US.

### MFK Karviná Expelled from Czech Football
The Ethics Committee of the Football Association (FAČR) expelled MFK Karviná after finding the club guilty of fixing three top-flight matches. The club was fined 10 million CZK, and Karviná mayor Jan Wolf received a 12-year football ban.

### Alcohol Addiction on the Rise
Up to 1 million Czechs (over 15) drink daily. More than 70% of the population uses addictive substances, according to the National Monitoring Center for Drugs and Addiction (NMS). Over half the population is considered at risk.

### CZK 300 Million Uber/Bolt Tax Fraud
Ten people charged in a massive tax fraud scheme operating through Uber and Bolt platforms via a network of shell companies. Total estimated damage to the state budget: CZK 300 million.

### Jiřího z Poděbrad Square Reopens
Prague's newly renovated Jiřího z Poděbrad square officially opened after a 3-year, 570-million-crown renovation project. Evening festivities marked the opening.
