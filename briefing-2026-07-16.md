## 🤖 AI & Technology

### NVIDIA's "Physical AI" Push Into Japan
Jensen Huang landed in Japan for the first time in ~9 months and is set to announce large-scale AI and robotics collaborations with the Japanese government and major corporations today, calling it **"the beginning of Japan AI."** The centerpiece is **Noetra**, a SoftBank-led sovereign AI project involving 44 Japanese companies (including Honda and NEC) and **¥1 trillion (~$6.2B)** in government subsidies to build a national-level "physical AI" model. NVIDIA is also partnering with Sega to bring its RTX Spark platform to the upcoming title *Virtua Fighter Crossroad*.

### UN Global Dialogue on AI Governance
The **inaugural UN Global Dialogue on AI Governance** opened in Geneva this week with delegates from **169 countries** — described as the most significant multilateral AI governance conversation to date, amid warnings from experts about the risk of "catastrophic harm" from unregulated systems.

### EU Driver-Monitoring Mandate
Since **July 7**, all newly registered cars in the EU must include an AI-based driver distraction detection system analyzing gaze and head movement — another quiet milestone for AI in everyday safety-critical systems.

### Enterprise AI: Pilot-to-Production Bottleneck
New IDC research highlights that many enterprises are stuck moving AI projects from pilot to production, with **networking infrastructure** cited as the main blocker even as pilot adoption accelerates.

### Also Notable
- Google Pixel 11 teaser confirms a new "Pixel Glow" feature ahead of pre-orders.
- Samsung unveiled new AI-connected slide-in ranges and over-the-range microwaves with "Air Fry Max."
- Google's Africa Applied AI Lab continues expanding early access to African researchers and entrepreneurs.

## 🇨🇿 Czech News

### NATO Summit Rift in Ankara
Czech PM **Andrej Babiš** and President **Petr Pavel** both attended the NATO summit in Ankara — but arrived on **separate planes**, sat at **different tables**, and largely avoided each other, prompting international coverage (including from CNN) of the unusual "two delegations" situation. The Constitutional Court had ordered the government to let Pavel attend after Foreign Minister Petr Macinka argued in January that Pavel had acted "outside the constitutional framework." Babiš confirmed **Czechia will not join the €70bn allied aid package for Ukraine**. Separately, new NATO data shows Czechia will hit its **2.01% of GDP** defense-spending target this year.

### Anger Over IOC Lifting Russia's Suspension
The IOC preliminarily lifted the **nearly three-year suspension** of the Russian Olympic Committee, clearing the way for Russian athletes to enter 2028 LA Olympic qualifiers. The **Czech Olympic Committee** and Sport Minister **Boris Šťastný** (Motoristé sobě) strongly condemned the move, calling it "good news for the Kremlin... bad news for the world" given Russia's continued war in Ukraine.

### Firefighters Deployed to Greek Wildfires
A Czech firefighting team has been dispatched to Greece to help battle wildfires threatening the region.

### Global Context Reaching Czech Markets
Renewed Iran-related fighting and closure of the Strait of Hormuz are pushing oil traders to warn of losing the market's "last energy security buffer" — a story being closely watched by Czech energy commentators given import exposure.
