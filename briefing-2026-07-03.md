## 🤖 AI & Technology

### Anthropic Ships Claude Sonnet 5, Fable 5 Returns
**Claude Sonnet 5** is now the default model for all Free and Pro users worldwide, with major gains in reasoning, tool use, and coding over Sonnet 4.6. Introductory API pricing ($2/$10 per million input/output tokens) runs through August 31 before standard pricing ($3/$15) kicks in. Separately, the export-restricted **Fable 5** model returned globally on July 1 at 3:31pm ET after a 19-day US export-control suspension — Commerce lifted the restriction June 30, and Anthropic added new classifiers to block cybersecurity misuse. Pro/Max/Team/Enterprise users get elevated usage limits through July 7.

### OpenAI Ships GPT-5.6 in Limited Preview
The three-tier **GPT-5.6 family (Sol, Terra, Luna)** launched on Cerebras hardware at up to 750 tokens/second, briefed to the US government pre-launch with the first partner wave coordinated with federal officials. Broader availability expected "in the coming weeks."

### Google: Gemini Spark Lands on Mac, 3.5 Pro Delayed
**Gemini Spark**, Google's agentic assistant, launched on Mac July 1 with real-time topic tracking and Google Tasks/Keep integration; the Gemini app has grown past **900 million monthly active users**. Meanwhile **Gemini 3.5 Pro's** general release has slipped again, now targeted for later in July.

### Nvidia's Vera Rubin Platform Hits Full Production
Seven new chips — Vera CPU, Rubin GPU, NVLink 6, ConnectX-9, BlueField-4, Spectrum-6, plus the newly integrated Groq 3 LPU — are now shipping to AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft, and OCI for H2 2026 deployment, promising up to 10x lower inference token cost versus Blackwell.

### June Jobs Report Shows AI's Labor-Market Bite Widening
The BLS report released July 3 showed just **57,000 jobs added in June** versus a 185,000 consensus estimate — the weakest month since 2024 — with tech layoffs hitting 142,000 year-to-date and an estimated 88,000 US job cuts directly attributed to AI in 2026.

### Other
- **Microsoft** debuted "Scout," an always-on autonomous work agent integrated with Teams/Outlook/M365, available via its Frontier preview program. Anthropic is reportedly in early talks with Microsoft to run Claude inference on Azure's custom Maia 200 chips.
- The **White House** is reportedly nearing a voluntary frontier-AI standards deal with major labs, with an announcement possibly landing the week of July 7.

## 🇨🇿 Czech News

### Parliament Strips President Pavel of Appointment Power
The Chamber of Deputies passed an SPD-backed amendment July 1 removing the president's authority to appoint and recall heads of Czech missions to NATO, the UN, EU, and UNESCO — shifting that power to the Foreign Ministry. Opposition figures are calling it a "lex pomsta" (revenge law); STAN's Vít Rakušan is weighing a constitutional complaint. The bill now moves to the Senate.

### Babiš-Pavel Feud Escalates Ahead of NATO Summit
PM Andrej Babiš said President Pavel's planned presence at the NATO summit in Ankara would "damage the Czech Republic's reputation," despite a Constitutional Court order requiring Pavel's inclusion in the delegation. Motorists party figure Filip Turek responded by suggesting the Constitutional Court "should not exist." The delegation, with Pavel included, departs for Ankara within days.

### New Army Chief Takes Over
President Pavel appointed **Lt. Gen. Miroslav Hlaváč** as the new Chief of General Staff on July 1, replacing Karel Řehka at a ceremonial parade at Prague-Vítkov marking Armed Forces Day.

### Explosives Cache Seized in Prague
Police detained a 39-year-old man in Prague's Březiněves after a three-month investigation, seizing **750 grams of Semtex** plastic explosive along with several suppressed pistols and ammunition.

### Record Heatwave Aftermath
Czechia set an all-time national temperature record of **41.9°C at Doksany** on June 28, smashing the 2012 record. ČHMÚ switched to a new impact-based severe-weather warning system on July 1.

### Karlovy Vary Film Festival Opens
KVIFF's 60th edition opens today, July 3, running through July 11 with 130+ feature films. The opening night features a 1,300-drone show — the largest ever staged in Czechia or Slovakia — replacing traditional fireworks.

## 🥊 MMA / UFC

### Justin Gaethje Named Fighter of the Midyear
Gaethje became the first fighter in UFC history to win two interim belts before upsetting Ilia Topuria at UFC Freedom 250 (White House South Lawn, June 14) to claim the undisputed lightweight title.

### New Meta UFC Rankings Shake Up the Top of the Sport
UFC and Meta launched a new Elo-based ranking system (debuted June 20) weighing opponent quality, activity, and recency. Gaethje jumped to No. 6 pound-for-pound; Manel Kape climbed into the flyweight top three.

### Usman Moves Up to Face Du Plessis
Former welterweight champ **Kamaru Usman** (39) faces former middleweight champ **Dricus Du Plessis** on July 18 in Oklahoma City — Usman's first fight at 185 lbs.

### Looking Ahead

- **UFC 330** (August 15, Philadelphia) added fights around the Islam Makhachev vs. Ian Machado Garry welterweight title headliner.

## 🚴 Cycling

### Tour de France 2026 Starts Tomorrow in Barcelona
The 113th Tour opens **July 4** with a 19.6km team time trial — the first Tour stage-opening TTT since 1971 — and runs through July 26, closing with Alpe d'Huez (Stage 20) and the Champs-Élysées (Stage 21). The finalized **startlist has 184 riders across 23 teams**.

### Pogačar the Favorite, Vingegaard Chasing a Giro-Tour Double
**Tadej Pogačar** enters chasing a fifth Tour title (third straight); at Thursday's press conference he name-checked teammate Isaac del Toro as another potential winner alongside acknowledging **Jonas Vingegaard** (fresh off winning the 2026 Giro d'Italia) as his main rival. Remco Evenepoel and Florian Lipowitz round out the top GC tier.

### Injuries Thin the Contender Field
**Wout van Aert** is out after an elbow infection from a crash required surgery — he's targeting the Vuelta a España instead. Netcompany-Ineos loses GC dark horse **Oscar Onley** (4th last year) to a shoulder injury, though teammate Josh Tarling recovered from a fractured collarbone in time to debut.

### Squads Locked In
Visma-Lease a Bike: Vingegaard, Jorgenson, Kuss, Campenaerts, Armirail, Affini, plus debutants Hagenes and Piganzoli. UAE Team Emirates-XRG: Pogačar, Del Toro, Großschartner, McNulty, Politt, Vermeersch, Wellens, Adam Yates. Netcompany-Ineos builds around 2019 champion Egan Bernal and TT specialist Filippo Ganna, notably omitting Carlos Rodríguez.
