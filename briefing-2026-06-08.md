## World Affairs

### Major Earthquake Strikes Philippines — Tsunami Alerts Across Asia
A powerful **magnitude 7.8 earthquake** struck off the island of Mindanao in the southern Philippines early Monday morning, killing at least 15 people and injuring more than 200. The USGS recorded the quake shortly before 7:40 a.m. local time.

**General Santos** (pop. 722,000) sustained the most serious damage — parts of St. Elizabeth Hospital were severely damaged, forcing patients to be evacuated and treated outdoors. More than an hour of aftershocks followed the initial quake.

Tsunami warnings were issued for the Philippines, Indonesia, and Japan. Philippine authorities ordered immediate evacuations in nine provinces, including Sarangani, Davao Occidental, Tawi-Tawi, and Sulu. The largest observed waves — around **0.83 metres** — hit Indonesia's Northern Sulawesi province.

### Iran War: Strait of Hormuz Standoff Continues
Now over three months since the US–Israeli strikes on Iran's leadership on **February 28**, the conflict has settled into a tense brinkmanship phase. A US–Iran ceasefire agreed on **April 7–8** largely holds, but ongoing disputes over restricted access to the **Strait of Hormuz** continue to disrupt global shipping and fuel supplies — particularly in parts of Asia. No new major military strikes were reported overnight.

## Politics & Governance

### Senate Passes Reconciliation Bill After All-Night Vote
The Senate passed a **~$70 billion reconciliation bill** funding immigration enforcement at 4:52 a.m. Monday in a 52–47 party-line vote. **Sen. Lisa Murkowski (R-AK)** was the sole Republican to vote against final passage.

Key controversies were resolved before the vote:
- The controversial **$1.8 billion "anti-weaponization" fund** — which critics said could compensate Jan. 6 rioters — was stripped after acting AG Todd Blanche said DOJ would not proceed with it.
- A **$1 billion White House ballroom** provision was also removed after bipartisan backlash.

The bill now heads back to the House for final approval.

### Congress Returns — Busy Week Ahead
Both chambers return Monday from a brief recess facing a packed agenda: the reconciliation bill, ICE and CBP appropriations, a FISA reauthorization, and the **Farm Bill**. GOP leaders are aiming to move quickly before the summer recess.

## Climate & Science

### El Niño Forecast Intensifies
Forecasters are increasingly confident a **strong El Niño** will develop between June and September 2026 and persist into early 2027. The **Climate Vulnerable Forum** warned of compounding shocks to food, water, and health systems layered on top of an already record-warm baseline. UN Secretary-General **António Guterres** again urged emergency preparedness globally.

The Philippines earthquake response will be further complicated if El Niño conditions bring drought to Mindanao — one of the regions already flagged as highly vulnerable.
