## World News

### Iran–US: Ceasefire Holds Tenuously as Trump Mulls 60-Day Extension
US and Iranian negotiators reached a preliminary memorandum to extend the ceasefire by 60 days and launch formal nuclear talks, but President Trump has not yet signed off. The proposed terms include Iran removing all mines from the Strait of Hormuz within 30 days, no tolls or harassment of shipping, and a proportional rollback of the US naval blockade. The deal remains fragile — Kuwait intercepted missiles fired from Iran earlier this week, and key sticking points persist around Iran's uranium stockpile, frozen assets, and the ongoing Lebanon conflict. Oil markets are swinging sharply on each headline.

### US Strikes Iran Again Amid Continuing Diplomacy
The US carried out additional strikes against Iranian targets even as peace talks continue, illustrating the unstable dual-track of escalation and negotiation. Israel and Lebanon officials are also set to hold talks today, adding another thread to an already complex regional picture.

### Romania: Russian Drone Crashes Into Apartment Building
A Russian drone launched in an overnight attack on Ukraine crossed into eastern Romania and crashed into an apartment building, injuring two people. Romania's foreign minister confirmed the drone was of Russian origin and was carrying explosives — a significant NATO incident that is drawing calls for stronger alliance response.

### Ebola Epicenter Emerges in Eastern DRC
WHO Director-General arrived in Kinshasa to assess Congo's latest Ebola outbreak. The epicenter is Mongbwalu, a gold-mining town of ~130,000 in Ituri province, eastern DRC. Dense population, limited healthcare infrastructure, and regional conflict are all hampering containment. The WHO head's visit signals elevated international concern.

### Shangri-La Dialogue Opens in Singapore
The annual Asia-Pacific security summit opened today, bringing defence ministers and officials together to address the Middle East crisis, Russia's war in Ukraine, and South China Sea tensions. The dialogue is expected to be unusually fraught given simultaneous flashpoints across multiple regions.

## Technology & AI

### Sam Altman Walks Back "Jobs Apocalypse" Forecast
Speaking at a Commonwealth Bank of Australia conference in Sydney, OpenAI CEO Sam Altman admitted his own earlier predictions on AI's economic impact were "pretty wrong." He acknowledged expecting more disruption to entry-level white-collar jobs than has actually materialised, and conceded there remains a "human part" of employment AI hasn't displaced. The remarks come weeks before OpenAI is expected to confidentially file for a US IPO targeting a **$1 trillion valuation** and at least **$60 billion** in fundraising. Note: tech layoffs in 2026 have already hit 115,000 through May, approaching all of 2025's total, with Meta, Amazon and Snap citing AI-driven restructuring.

### Pope Leo XIV Calls for AI Regulation
In his first encyclical, *Magnifica Humanitas*, Pope Leo XIV issued a sweeping moral call for stronger regulation of artificial intelligence. The Vatican warned that AI risks concentrating power, distorting truth, reshaping labour markets, and lowering the threshold for warfare. The document is the Catholic Church's most comprehensive statement on technology in decades.

### DuckDuckGo Surges 30% on Privacy Backlash Against Google AI Search
DuckDuckGo reported a **30% increase** in app and browser installs, driven by users seeking alternatives to Google's AI-generated search results. Privacy concerns and dissatisfaction with AI-summary quality are fuelling the shift, putting pressure on Google's search monopoly.

### AI Code Generation Sparks Cybersecurity Hiring Wave
Demand for cybersecurity professionals is accelerating as AI-generated code introduces new classes of vulnerabilities faster than teams can patch them. The World Economic Forum has identified AI as the **#1 driver of global cyber risk in 2026**, with 94% of organisations citing it as their primary concern.

### EdTech Under Scrutiny
US schools are rolling back classroom device programmes after years of heavy spending, amid growing evidence that screen saturation is harming learning quality. The reassessment is the most significant pullback in edtech adoption in over a decade.
