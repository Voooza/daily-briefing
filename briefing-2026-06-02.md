## World News

### Iran-US War: Talks Resume After Iran Threatens "Other Fronts"
President Trump posted on Truth Social Monday that indirect nuclear talks with Iran are continuing at a **"rapid pace"** — hours after an IRGC-linked Iranian outlet announced the regime was suspending negotiations and threatening to open "other fronts" in the war. Iran cited alleged ceasefire violations by the US and Israel as justification. Al Jazeera reports the US struck Iranian radar and drone-control infrastructure after Tehran shot down an American MQ-1 Predator drone over the weekend. Both sides appear to be simultaneously fighting and negotiating, with analysts describing the talks as fragile.

### Lebanon: Offensive Deepens Despite Ceasefire Talk
Israel's military expanded its ground push into Lebanon — its deepest invasion in two decades — and captured the 12th-century **Beaufort Castle**, a strategic hilltop fortress overlooking southern Lebanon and northern Israel. Trump stated "all shooting will stop" following calls with both sides, but Israeli PM Netanyahu later said the military would **"continue to operate as planned"** in southern Lebanon. Lebanon's health ministry counts at least **3,433 killed** and **10,395 wounded** since the war began on March 2. Over 1 million people (20%+ of Lebanon's population) have been displaced. France is pushing for an emergency UN Security Council session.

### Colombia: Runoff Set After Surprise Result
Tough-on-crime outsider **Aberaldo de la Espriella** took the lead in Sunday's first-round presidential vote, setting up a June runoff against **Iván Cepeda**, an ally of outgoing President Gustavo Petro. The outcome will shape regional trade, drug policy, and Venezuela relations.

### Congo: Bundibugyo Virus Outbreak Tops 1,000 Cases
The Democratic Republic of Congo has reported over **1,000 suspected cases** of the Bundibugyo virus, a rare and deadly hemorrhagic fever for which there is no approved treatment or vaccine. Health authorities are scrambling to contain the outbreak.

### Ethiopia Heads to the Polls
Ethiopia is preparing for national elections. Multiple parties and coalitions are contesting in what observers are watching as a test of the country's political stability under Prime Minister Abiy Ahmed.

## US Domestic

### California Governor Primary — Polls Open, No Clear Dem Frontrunner
Today's top-two primary for California governor could produce a historic outcome: with **61 candidates** on the ballot and polls showing **Xavier Becerra (D, 23%)** and Republican **Steve Hilton (20%)** leading, followed closely by Tom Steyer (D, 15%), Chad Bianco (R, 13%), and Katie Porter (D, 12%), there is a real possibility that two Republicans advance to the general — shutting Democrats out of the race for the nation's most populous state in a midterm year. Results are expected tonight; the official count will continue through the canvass period.

## Technology & AI

### Anthropic Confidentially Files for IPO at ~$965B Valuation
Anthropic submitted a draft Form S-1 to the SEC on June 1, marking one of the most anticipated public offerings in history. The company raised **$65 billion** in a Series H round that pushed its valuation to **$965 billion** — narrowly surpassing rival OpenAI. Revenue run-rate has hit **$47 billion**, up from $9 billion at end-2025. If Anthropic debuts at $1 trillion, it would rank among the two or three largest IPOs ever. Share count and price are not yet set; the SEC review must be completed before a date is confirmed.

### Nvidia +2%: New AI PC Chip Announced
Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang unveiled a new chip targeting the consumer PC market, positioning it as bringing home computers into the AI era. Markets responded positively, with **Nvidia shares up ~2%** and the announcement helping push the Nasdaq to a new record above 27,000.

### Pancreatic Cancer Drug May Help Multiple Cancers
Researchers are expanding trials of **daraxonrasib**, the experimental oral drug that recently showed "unprecedented" results doubling survival in advanced pancreatic cancer patients. Oncologists are now investigating its potential against lung, colon, and ovarian cancers — raising hopes for a broader class of hard-to-treat tumors.
