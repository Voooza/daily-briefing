## 🤖 AI & Technology

### The Biggest AI Day of the Year
Today is shaping up as the single busiest date on the 2026 AI calendar. Google is *rumored* to launch **Gemini 3.5 Pro** today after scrapping its original base model and restarting pretraining — leaked specs point to a **2-million-token context window**, a new "Deep Think" reasoning layer, and pricing near $1.25/$10 per million input/output tokens. Caveat: as of this week no model card, pricing page, or API listing had gone public, so this remains a leak, not a confirmed launch.

### Shanghai World AI Conference Opens
Shanghai's **2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference** (WAIC) kicks off today and runs through July 20, with **140+ forums** and **1,100+ exhibitors**. Notably, **Xi Jinping** is attending in person for the first time since the event launched in 2018 — a signal Beijing is elevating AI governance and industrial policy to a top national priority.

### OpenAI Pitches US Government a Stake
**Sam Altman** has reportedly proposed giving the US government a **5% stake in OpenAI** (worth roughly **$42.6B** at its recent $852B valuation), pitching the idea directly to President Trump, Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick, and Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent.

## 🇨🇿 Czech News

### Coalition Friction Over Ukraine Aid
SPD chairman and Chamber of Deputies speaker **Tomio Okamura** has said he fundamentally disagrees with Czech contributions to financing weapons for Ukraine and has asked coalition partners not to repeat such commitments — the latest sign of strain within the Babiš government over Ukraine policy.

### Czech Republic Loses Two Cases at European Court of Human Rights
The **ECHR** ruled against the Czech Republic in two cases stemming from 2021, including one involving the death of a young Romani man following a police intervention in Teplice while he was under the influence of drugs.

### Pavel Distances Himself From Kolář
President **Petr Pavel** told MF DNES that former diplomat **Petr Kolář** "does not have the status of advisor" and has no contract with Prague Castle, pushing back on reports characterizing Kolář as a close presidential adviser.

### Weather: Heatwave Building, Storms Friday
Forecasters expect temperatures to climb toward **30°C nationally** and up to **33°C in Moravia and Silesia** by Friday, with fire-danger warnings still active in southern Moravia. Strong storms are expected to arrive over the weekend, potentially bringing ~40mm of rain, hail, and damaging wind gusts.

## 🚴 Cycling

### Tour de France — Stage 12: Merlier's Hat-Trick
**Tim Merlier** won a chaotic, crash-marred **Stage 12** (Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours → Chalon-sur-Saône, 179 km) for his **third stage win** of this Tour, out-sprinting Olav Kooij and Jasper Philipsen after Lidl-Trek tore the race apart late. A high-speed pile-up involving **Fernando Gaviria** and **Søren Wærenskjold** marred the finish. The **general classification held**: **Tadej Pogačar** leads by **3:36** over **Jonas Vingegaard**, with **Remco Evenepoel** third.

### Today: Stage 13 — Longest Stage of the Tour
**Stage 13** (Dole → Belfort, **205.8 km**) is the longest stage of the 2026 race and the week's last breakaway opportunity. The route stays flat for 150km before tackling the Cat. 3 **Col des Croix** and then the historic **Ballon d'Alsace** (8.7km at 6.9%, up to 8.8%) — the very first mountain ever climbed in Tour history, 121 years ago — with its descent as the finale. The intermediate sprint passes through **Mélisey**, hometown of retired French climber Thibaut Pinot.
