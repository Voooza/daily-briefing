## Politics & Governance

### House Passes $70B ICE Funding Package
The House approved a **$70 billion immigration enforcement and border security** package, locking in ICE funding through the end of the Trump presidency and ending a months-long standoff with Democrats over immigration spending priorities.

### California Governor Primary: Hilton and Becerra Advance
With ballot counting ongoing, Republican **Steve Hilton** (28%) and Democrat **Xavier Becerra** (26%) have broken ahead of the field in California's all-party jungle primary and are set to face each other in November. Billionaire Democrat **Tom Steyer** — who self-funded to the tune of $213 million — sits in third at 20% and is effectively eliminated. Hilton, a former Fox News host backed by Trump, ran on affordability; Becerra surged after frontrunner Eric Swalwell exited in April amid misconduct allegations.

### Trump-Backed Hilton vs. Former Biden Cabinet Member
The California November matchup shapes up as one of the year's most watched governor's races: Trump's endorsed candidate against Biden's former HHS/AG secretary in the nation's most populous state.

### Nevada Governor Race Set
Democratic Attorney General **Aaron Ford** will challenge Republican Governor **Joe Lombardo** in November, setting up one of 2026's most competitive gubernatorial contests.

## World Affairs

### US Strikes Iran — Again; Iran Retaliates Across the Region
The conflict with Iran sharply escalated overnight. A **US Army Apache helicopter** was shot down over the Strait of Hormuz — both pilots were rescued safely by drone boat, a first for US forces. President Trump confirmed strikes in retaliation, and **CENTCOM declared the operations "completed."** Iran's Revolutionary Guard fired back, claiming **21 attacks on US air and naval bases** across the region, including strikes on Al Azraq air base in Jordan (targeting F-35 hangars), Bahrain, and Kuwait. An MQ-9 Reaper drone was also reportedly shot down. Before the escalation, Trump had said a deal was in **"the final throes"** — only to issue fresh strike threats hours later.

### Trump: Deal "Two or Three Days Away" — Then Strikes Anyway
Markets whiplashed as Trump first suggested a diplomatic breakthrough was imminent, then threatened further strikes — a cycle that pushed chip stocks lower and sent Treasury yields higher before markets partially recovered.

### Xi Jinping Visits Pyongyang
Chinese President **Xi Jinping** traveled to North Korea in a move analysts interpret as Beijing reasserting its influence over Pyongyang amid shifting geopolitical dynamics tied to the Iran conflict and US-China tensions.

### Philippines Earthquake — 32 Dead
A **magnitude 7.8 earthquake** struck offshore in the southern Philippines, killing at least **32 people**, injuring more than 200, and generating a roughly 1-metre (3-foot) tsunami that hit nearby coastlines. Rescue operations are ongoing.

### UK: Anti-Immigration Protests Flare After Belfast Stabbing
British leaders called for calm after the arrest of a Sudanese man accused of a near-fatal stabbing in Belfast sparked **fiery anti-immigration demonstrations** across the UK. Police are managing protest activity in multiple cities.

### Pope Leo XIV Blesses Sagrada Família's Completed Tower
In a landmark moment, **Pope Leo XIV** celebrated a Solemn Mass at Barcelona's Sagrada Família and blessed the newly completed **Tower of Jesus Christ** — at 172.5 metres (566 ft), now the world's tallest church spire. The ceremony falls exactly **100 years to the day since architect Antoni Gaudí's death** on June 10, 1926. Earlier in the day the Pope visited Montserrat abbey and met with prisoners.

## Technology & AI

### Anthropic Confidentially Files IPO with SEC
**Anthropic** — the AI company behind Claude — confidentially submitted a draft S-1 registration to the SEC on June 1, signaling a potential public offering pending SEC review and market conditions. The company's revenue run rate has reached **$47 billion**, up from $10 billion last year, and its most recent funding round pegged its valuation at **$965 billion** — narrowly topping rival OpenAI's $852 billion valuation. Anthropic is racing to list ahead of OpenAI, which is also preparing its own confidential filing.
