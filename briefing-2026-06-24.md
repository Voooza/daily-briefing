## 🤖 Artificial Intelligence

**ChatGPT crosses 1 billion monthly active users** — OpenAI's flagship product has passed the symbolic milestone of one billion monthly active users, cementing AI assistants as mass-market technology.

**US–China AI tech war escalates** — Ten days after Washington restricted Anthropic's top models from foreign markets, Beijing retaliated by blacklisting 56 American tech firms. China has also raised $7.4 billion to accelerate its domestic AI push. Microsoft's CEO warned that letting "a few models eat everything" is politically unsustainable in the long run.

**Trump signs new AI executive order** — The White House published an executive order titled *"Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security,"* dramatically shifting the administration's stance. The order cuts regulatory red tape to accelerate AI growth while establishing new national-security guardrails across agencies.

**AI Gigafactory in space** — The race to build AI data centers in orbit is gaining momentum, with companies eyeing abundant solar power and the elimination of terrestrial environmental challenges as key advantages.

**Samsung Health gets major AI overhaul** — Samsung announced AI-powered features for its Health app: Vitals, Daily Cardio Load, Heart Health Score, and Fitness Index.

**Trump signs quantum computing executive orders** — Two new executive orders aim to cement US leadership in quantum computing: one protecting against advanced cryptographic attacks, another encouraging commercial and scientific quantum innovation.

## 🇨🇿 Czech Republic

**Public media 24-hour warning strike** — Staff at Czech Television and Czech Radio staged a warning strike Monday against a government plan to shift their funding to the state budget. Unions called the proposal "liquidating" and warned of further, more disruptive action unless the government withdraws the legislation.

**AI Gigafactory approved** — The Czech government greenlit infrastructure for a large AI data centre ("AI Gigafactory") to be built domestically. Industry Minister Karel Havlíček (ANO) framed it as key to national security and AI independence.

**Pavel vs. Babiš government over NATO summit** — President Petr Pavel has appealed to the Constitutional Court after the government denied his request to lead the Czech delegation to the upcoming NATO summit in Ankara — a significant constitutional dispute over presidential vs. executive authority.

**Heatwave incoming** — The Czech Hydrometeorological Institute (ČHMÚ) is warning of record-high temperatures expected to peak this weekend, with some models forecasting highs up to **40°C**. Thunderstorms also possible.

**Economic diplomacy conference** — PM Andrej Babiš and Foreign Minister Petr Macinka opened a five-day working conference at Czernin Palace gathering Czech economic, agricultural, and science diplomats from embassies worldwide, focused on advancing Czech economic interests abroad.

## 🚴 Cycling

**Tour de France 2026 roster deadline passes** — Teams submitted their official 8-rider rosters by the June 23–24 deadline. The race starts **July 4 in Barcelona** with a team time trial Grand Départ.
