## World News

### Iran Strikes Kuwait Airport, Attacks Bahrain — US Retaliates on Qeshm Island
In one of the most serious exchanges since the April ceasefire began, Iran launched ballistic missiles and drones at Kuwait and Bahrain overnight. A drone struck the **passenger terminal at Kuwait International Airport**, causing injuries and significant damage; two additional missiles aimed at Kuwait fell short or broke apart en route. Three missiles fired at Bahrain were intercepted by US and Bahraini air-defence forces, with US Central Command reporting no American casualties. The US responded with strikes on an Iranian military ground control station on **Qeshm Island** — Iran's main oil hub in the Strait of Hormuz — calling the action "defensive." Negotiations remain fragile: Iran's foreign ministry separately announced sanctions on the US's largest cryptocurrency exchange as part of its counter-pressure campaign, while American diplomats say back-channel talks are continuing.

### Israel-Lebanon: Washington Talks Yield 45-Day Ceasefire Extension
A fourth round of peace talks concluded in Washington on June 2–3, with Israel and Lebanon agreeing to a **45-day extension** of their ceasefire after two days described as "productive" by the US State Department. Deputy National Security Adviser **Mike Needham** led the American delegation. Key sticking points — Israeli troop withdrawal from southern Lebanon and Hezbollah disarmament — remain unresolved and will be taken up in the next round. Lebanon's health ministry figures remain at approximately 3,400 killed since hostilities began in March.

## US Domestic

### California Governor Primary: Hilton and Becerra Head to November
With more than half of ballots counted from Tuesday's top-two primary, Republican **Steve Hilton** holds a narrow one-point lead over Democrat **Xavier Becerra** (27% vs 26%), with billionaire Tom Steyer in third at roughly 15%. Candidates including Katie Porter, Antonio Villaraigosa, and Matt Mahan have already conceded. Because California uses a blanket primary, the two leaders advance regardless of party — meaning the November race will be a **Republican vs. Democrat** contest. Final results may take until mid-June given California's mail-ballot counting rules.

### Sanders Introduces American AI Sovereign Wealth Fund Act
Senator **Bernie Sanders** announced legislation that would impose a one-time **50% tax on the largest US AI companies — paid in stock** — to seed a public sovereign wealth fund. Named targets include OpenAI, Anthropic, and xAI. The fund would hold voting shares, have equal board representation, and distribute cash proceeds directly to Americans. Sanders published an op-ed in the New York Times framing the measure: *"AI, trained on the collective knowledge and labor of Americans without consent, must share its rewards with the public rather than a handful of billionaires."* The bill faces long odds in the current Senate but is drawing significant commentary ahead of Anthropic's pending IPO.

## Technology & AI

### Trump Signs Executive Order on AI Innovation and Security
President Trump signed an Executive Order directing expanded funding for AI vulnerability detection and new cybersecurity hiring pathways, positioning the US to compete with China in the deployment of autonomous AI systems. The order broadly endorses industry-led standards rather than mandatory regulation.

### Google Upgrades Search with Gemini 3.5 Flash
Google announced at its post-I/O rollout that **Gemini 3.5 Flash** — its newest frontier model optimised for agents and coding — is becoming the default model in AI Mode globally. The company highlighted agentic capabilities that can execute multi-step tasks across the web without user intervention.

### Alphabet Plans $80 Billion Stock Sale for AI Infrastructure
Alphabet is set to sell new shares for the first time since 2005, aiming to raise **$80 billion** in fresh equity earmarked for AI data centres and compute capacity. The announcement reflects growing pressure from cloud competitors and investor expectations around sustained AI capital expenditure.

### Q1 Earnings Season Closes at Record Margin
FactSet's final blended figures show Q1 S&P 500 net profits grew **28.6%** year-over-year — more than double the 13.1% analysts expected entering the quarter — with net profit margin hitting **14.8%**, the highest since tracking began in 2009.
