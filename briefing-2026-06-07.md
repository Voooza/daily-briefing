## Politics & Governance

### US: House Returns Monday for Reconciliation Vote
Congress resumes on Monday with the **reconciliation bill** as the primary item, expected to pass the House. The bill covers key fiscal provisions Republicans have been advancing since April.

### California Governor Primary Heats Up
The California governor primary continues ahead of the November election. Leading candidates include Democrats **Katie Porter**, **Tom Steyer**, and **Xavier Becerra**, and Republicans **Steve Hilton** and **Chad Bianco** — all vying to replace term-limited Gov. Gavin Newsom.

### Iowa Senate Race Set
In Iowa, Rep. **Ashley Hinson** won the Republican nomination to fill the Senate seat vacated by **Sen. Joni Ernst**. She will face Democratic state Rep. **Josh Turek** in November.

### Senate Rejects Jan. 6 Fund Block
Senators defeated an amendment that would have barred the White House from creating a **$1.8 billion fund** that critics said could be used to compensate Jan. 6 rioters.

## World Affairs

### Iran Escalates — Missiles Hit Kuwait and Bahrain
Iran fired **seven ballistic missiles** at Kuwait and Bahrain early Saturday in what Gulf states are calling a fresh act of "blatant aggression." Six missiles were intercepted; the seventh fell short of its target. No casualties were reported, though shrapnel caused material damage. This is the second missile salvo against both countries in three days — following a drone strike on Kuwait International Airport on June 3 that killed one and wounded 63.

The US-Iran conflict, now in its fourth month since US and Israeli strikes on Iran's leadership on **February 28**, remains far from resolution. No progress in diplomatic negotiations has been reported, with Iran's Foreign Minister Araghchi confirming talks are stalled.

### Pope Leo XIV: Iran War Is Not a "Just War"
**Pope Leo XIV** publicly stated that the ongoing war involving Iran does not meet the criteria of a "just war" under Catholic teaching, adding a high-profile moral voice to calls for de-escalation.

### Lebanon Aid Crisis Deepens
The UN reports **1.4 million people** are now in need of humanitarian aid in Lebanon amid continued Israeli military operations. International relief agencies are struggling to maintain supply access.

### Albania: "Flamingo Revolution" Spreads
Days of protests erupted across Albania over a **Jared Kushner-backed luxury coastal resort** project on the Albanian Riviera. Demonstrators under the banner of the "Flamingo Revolution" are demanding the government cancel what critics call an environmentally destructive giveaway of public coastline.

### Indian Gen Z Protests Hit New Delhi
Inspired by earlier movements, hundreds of **Gen Z protesters** rallied in New Delhi, demanding the resignation of India's education minister. The demonstrators adopted the rallying cry *"I'm a cockroach"* — a defiant symbol of resilience against institutional pressure.

### BYD Enters Humanoid Robotics
Chinese EV giant **BYD** confirmed its entry into the humanoid robotics sector, leveraging its existing strengths in batteries, sensors, and AI software. The move signals deepening convergence between the automotive and advanced robotics industries.

### Spain Launches First Commercial Robotaxi Service
**Uber, WeRide, and AVOMO** announced the launch of Spain's first commercial robotaxi service, with initial deployment planned in the **Madrid region** — a milestone for autonomous mobility in Europe.

### AI in Healthcare: Eye Disease Detection
French startup **Zenkolab** launched an AI system that analyzes retinal images to detect eye diseases at early stages, aiming to expand diagnostic access to underserved areas lacking specialists.

### Ramp Valued at $44 Billion on AI Expansion

## Climate & Science

### El Niño Warning: 18 Months of Compounding Risk Ahead
A **strong El Niño event** is forecast to develop in the tropical Pacific between June and September 2026, persisting into early 2027. The **Climate Vulnerable Forum** warns of compounding shocks to food, water, health, and fiscal systems on top of an already record-warm baseline. UN Secretary-General **António Guterres** urged governments to treat El Niño as an "urgent climate warning."

### World Environment Day Sounds the Alarm
World Environment Day (June 5) drew global attention to the fact that the **past eleven years** have each been record-warmest on record. Calls intensified for stronger national climate commitments ahead of year-end negotiations.

### Trump Administration Dismantles Ocean Observation Network
The Trump administration moved to **shut down a decade-old deep-ocean observation network** — 900+ instruments across five Pacific and Atlantic arrays — used to track marine heatwaves, ocean acidification, and deep-sea ecosystems. Scientists warned the decision creates irreplaceable gaps in climate monitoring.

### Data Centers Now Use 448 TWh Annually
A new **UN report** finds global data centers consumed approximately **448 terawatt-hours** of electricity in 2025 — equivalent to more than the entire annual electricity consumption of several mid-sized nations — raising urgent questions about the sustainability of AI infrastructure buildout.
