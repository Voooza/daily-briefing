## 🤖 AI & Technology

### Fable 5 Free Access Expires Tonight
Anthropic's free access window for **Claude Fable 5** — extended for a third time amid competition from GPT-5.6 Sol — closes tonight at **11:59 PM PT**. Users will be routed to Opus 5, a possible fourth extension, or a credit-based option. Separately, Anthropic is reportedly moving closer to a **mega-IPO**, with bankers lining up investor meetings.

### Gemini 3.5 Pro Slips Again
Google's flagship **Gemini 3.5 Pro** missed its July 17 target for the third time, particularly lagging on coding benchmarks; Google is now exploring a **Gemini 3.6 Flash** stopgap release. Compounding the strain, Google reportedly ran short of compute and began **rationing capacity to Meta**, delaying some of Meta's internal AI projects. On the upside, Gemini added roughly **550 million monthly users in 13 months** and — alongside Claude — is one of only two major AI apps still growing DAUs every month this year, while ChatGPT's daily actives fell and Copilot's dropped ~60% year-to-date.

### OpenAI Offers US Government a $42.6B Stake
Sam Altman reportedly pitched Trump, Commerce Secretary Lutnick, and Treasury Secretary Bessent directly on giving the US government a stake in OpenAI worth **$42.6 billion**. Separately, OpenAI is **delaying the full public launch of GPT-5.6** after the government requested early access and additional oversight.

### Chip Sector: TSMC Soars, SoftBank Slides
**TSMC's Q2 net income jumped 77.4%** year-on-year, and it raised full-year revenue guidance above 40% growth while committing another **$100 billion to Arizona** fabs. Meanwhile, **SoftBank sank over 9%** as Asia chip stocks tracked a Wall Street AI-sector sell-off, and Alibaba/Baidu shares jumped in Hong Kong on news of an Apple AI partnership.

### Also Notable
- NVIDIA's Jensen Huang continues his Japan push, unveiling a new AI model alongside the "Noetra" sovereign-AI initiative.
- New York Gov. Hochul's AI data-center moratorium drew criticism from Trump.
- Amazon's senior cloud executive departed after 18 years with the company.

## 🇨🇿 Czech News

### Fuel Price Regulation Ends Today
The temporary **price regulation and diesel excise-tax exemption** — introduced in response to the Strait of Hormuz crisis — expired at midnight on Sunday, July 19. From Monday, July 20, gasoline and diesel prices in Czechia return to normal **market pricing**.

### Babiš and Macinka Both Absent From Cabinet Session
PM **Andrej Babiš** was in Paris for a **Coalition of the Willing** meeting while Foreign Minister **Petr Macinka** headed to Israel, leaving both out of Sunday's government session. Ministers still in attendance (led by Deputy PM **Alena Schillerová**) discussed a revision of **defense and crisis legislation**, preparations for **Expo 2027**, and a contract with British firm **Rolls-Royce SMR** covering small modular reactor sites at the former coal plants in **Dětmarovice** and **Tušimice** — part of ČEZ's broader SMR partnership, which targets a first Czech reactor at Temelín in the early 2030s.

### Half-Year Government Scorecard
The Babiš government also used the week to present its **first-half 2026 progress report**, saying it is successfully delivering on its policy program.

## 🚴 Cycling

### Tour de France — Pogačar Extends Grip on Stage 14
**Tadej Pogačar** soloed to his **fourth stage win** of this Tour, attacking on the Col du Haag before powering alone to the line at **Le Markstein** in the Vosges (4:00:07). **Isaac del Toro** took 2nd (+38s) and **Paul Seixas** 3rd, with the French teenager's bonus seconds moving him into the young-riders' lead. **Jonas Vingegaard** finished 4th (+44s) but now trails Pogačar by **4:30** overall, with **Remco Evenepoel** defending 3rd on GC against a fast-rising Seixas.

### Today: Stage 15 — First-Ever Finish at Plateau de Solaison
Stage 15 (184 km, ~4,700m of climbing) features a brand-new, unprecedented summit finish atop the **Plateau de Solaison** — an 11.3 km, 9.1%-average hors-catégorie climb — expected to shake up the GC battle behind Pogačar before the Tour's second rest day.
