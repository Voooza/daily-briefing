## 🤖 AI & Technology

### SpaceX Goes Public
SpaceX makes its Nasdaq debut today under ticker **SPCX**, following a record **$75 billion IPO** at a fixed $135/share offer price — one of the biggest tech listings in history.

### OpenAI Dreaming V3 Rolls Out
OpenAI launched its most significant memory upgrade since the original ChatGPT — the **Dreaming V3 architecture** — now reaching ChatGPT Plus and Pro users in the US. ChatGPT has also crossed the symbolic **1 billion monthly active users** milestone, though its global web traffic share has dropped from 76.5% (Feb 2025) to **54.7%**. Google Gemini is surging in second at **27.4%**, up ~104% in six months.

### Trump Signs AI Executive Order
President Trump signed an Executive Order on June 2 aimed at keeping the US as the **global AI leader**, directing federal agencies to strengthen cybersecurity capabilities, establish an AI cybersecurity clearinghouse, and develop classified benchmarks for assessing advanced AI models' cyber capabilities.

### Meta's Wearable AI Push
Meta is developing a broader wearable AI strategy including an **AI-powered pendant** and a business-focused "Wearables for Work" line, alongside expanding its AI glasses portfolio.

### Langflow Vulnerability Exploited
A high-severity path-traversal flaw (**CVE-2026-5027**) in Langflow — a popular agentic AI workflow platform — is being actively exploited in the wild. If you use it, patch now.

### World's First Wind-Powered Underwater Data Center
China launched an underwater data center near Shanghai, developed by HiCloud Technology, with **24 MW initial capacity** and seawater cooling — powered entirely by wind energy.

### Colorado AI Act Effective June 30
The **Colorado Consumer Protections for Artificial Intelligence Act** takes effect at month's end, requiring risk management programs, annual impact assessments, and disclosure obligations for high-risk AI systems.

## 🇨🇿 Czech News

- **Defense spending:** Czech Defense Minister Zůna is pushing for the defense budget to reach **2% of GDP** next year. The Czech Army recruited ~1,400 new soldiers in the first five months of 2026, on track to exceed its annual target.
- **Chinese cars banned from military areas:** The Ministry of Defense has started restricting **Chinese-made vehicles** from sensitive military zones over cyber-espionage concerns.
- **Ukrainian refugees:** Czechia backed an EU proposal to extend temporary protection for Ukrainian refugees beyond 2027, though with limits for men of military age. Czechia — which hosts the **most Ukrainian refugees per capita in the EU** — remains exempt from mandatory redistribution.
- **Inflation at 2.1%:** Czech annual inflation slowed to **2.1% in May**, the seventh lowest in Europe. Gastronomy prices, however, have surged **56% since 2019**.
- **Education reform:** From September 2028, Czech primary schools will no longer be required to teach English from grade 1 or a second language from grade 7.
- **Czech Television strike:** Staff plan a **warning strike on June 22** over proposed state budget financing changes.

## 🚴 Cycling

### Tour de Suisse — Starting June 17
The **89th Tour de Suisse** (June 17–21) is just around the corner — a shortened 5-stage format to attract Tour de France contenders. Key details:

- **First time starting in Italy** (opening stage around Sondrio)
- **Queen stage** finishes in Villars-sur-Ollon with 4,200m of climbing over repeated ascents of the Col de la Croix
- **First UCI WorldTour race with full gender parity** — men's and women's races run in parallel daily
- **Favourites:** Tadej Pogacar (never won TdS, clear favourite) and Mathieu van der Poel (returning after Paris-Roubaix, targeting stage wins)
